About This Curriculum

Why we built the most comprehensive, free, technical curriculum on Climate Tech and Smart Cities.

The Philosophy

We believe that the intersection of climate science, urbanization, and artificial intelligence represents the most critical engineering frontier of the 21st century. However, software engineers and computer science students often lack a structured pathway into this domain.

Existing resources are either too high-level (focused on policy without technical depth) or too academic (dense physics papers inaccessible to those without specific domain training).

This curriculum bridges that gap. It takes you from "absolute zero" to understanding how sophisticated AI models like GraphCast forecast global weather, how reinforcement learning optimizes energy grids, and how digital twins simulate modern megacities.

Our Promise: No filler. No marketing speak. We cite actual peer-reviewed papers throughout, discuss the complex algorithms powering solutions, and are brutally honest about the limitations of technology in solving what is fundamentally also a political and human challenge.

Who Should Take This Course?

  • Computer Science Students: Looking for a meaningful application of your skills beyond ad-tech and standard SaaS.
  • Machine Learning Engineers: Wanting to pivot into climate modeling, forecasting, or Earth observation.
  • Software Engineers: Interested in building the platforms that will manage distributed energy and circular economies.
  • Aspiring Founders: Searching for complex, high-impact problems to solve.

How It Was Built

This entire platform is built with a commitment to performance, accessibility, and minimal environmental footprint. It uses zero heavy frontend frameworks—only semantic HTML5, pure CSS3, and vanilla JavaScript. By eliminating unnecessary payloads, the site loads instantly, even on low-bandwidth connections globally.

The content synthesizes knowledge from the IPCC AR6 reports, seminal papers aggregated by Climate Change AI, and extensive real-world case studies from cities worldwide.
